## The case for editorial design

Most developer portfolios look the same: gradient backgrounds, glowing cards, and a grid of project thumbnails. They communicate competence but rarely communicate *taste*.

Editorial design-borrowed from magazines, publications, and platforms like Read.cv-prioritizes typography, whitespace, and narrative rhythm. For technical products, this approach makes complex work feel approachable and premium.

## Typography as hierarchy

Serif headings paired with clean sans-serif body text create natural visual hierarchy without boxes or borders. The reader's eye follows type size and weight, not container boundaries.

## Motion with purpose

Every animation should answer a question: Where should I look next? What changed? Scroll-driven reveals, gentle image zooms, and typography motion feel luxurious when they're sparse and intentional.

## What I'm exploring

In my own portfolio rewrite, I'm applying these principles to case studies-treating each project like an article cover with depth underneath. The goal isn't to impress with effects, but to make recruiters and clients feel like they're reading the work of someone who thinks carefully about craft.
